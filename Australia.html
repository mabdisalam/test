Australia's Great Barrier Reef is the largest reef structure on the planet

It stretches for over 1400 miles and is home to a very diverse selection of marine creatures such as fish, turtles, dolphins, sharks and even crocodiles!

The reef itself is a living animal and is made up of thousands of varieties of soft and hard corals. It grows at a rate of about 5mm per year.

It is believed that rising sea levels and temperatures could harm the reef and its inhabitants.
